Definitions

Attendances:
The number of patients recorded as arriving at a public emergency department.

Admissions:
The number of patients who are subsequently admitted to the hospital for care and/or treatment.

Triage: Categories allocated to each patient based on an assessment of their presenting conditions.

Standard deviation:
A quantity expressing by how much the members of a group differ from the mean value for the group.

"Fair":
When the probability is MORE THAN 50% that the number of attendances will exceed 0.5 standard deviations BELOW the mean (same as when the "chance of coffee" (see below) is above a threshold of 0.5).

"Average":
When the probability is LESS THAN 50% that the number of attendances will exceed 0.5 standard deviations above the mean AND When the probability is less than 50% that the number of attendances is less than 0.5 standard deviations below the mean average.

"Busy":
When the probability is MORE THAN 50% that the number of attendances will exceed 0.5 standard deviations ABOVE the mean (same as when the "chance of chaos" (see below) is above a threshold of 0.5).

Chance of admissions:
On average, to proportion of attendances that convert to inpatient stays.

"Chance of coffee":
The probability that the average attendances will be less than 0.5 standard deviations.

"Chance of chaos":
The probability that the average attendances will be more than 0.5 standard deviations.
